Red wine

Red wine is a type of wine that is made from red or black grapes. During the winemaking process, the grapes are crushed and the skins, seeds, and juice are fermented together, which gives the wine its characteristic red color. The longer the skins are left in contact with the juice, the darker the color of the resulting wine.

White wine

White wine is a type of wine that is made from white or green grapes or from the juice of red grapes with the skins removed. Unlike red wine, the skins are not fermented with the juice, resulting in a lighter color and less tannins in the wine.

Rosé wine

Rosé wine is a type of wine that is made from red grapes, but the skins are removed after only a short period of time (typically a few hours to a couple of days), resulting in a pinkish color. Rosé wine can also be made by blending red and white wines, although this is less common. Rosé wine can range from dry to sweet and can have a variety of flavor profiles depending on the grapes and winemaking techniques used.

Dry wine

Dry wines are wines that have little to no residual sugar left after the fermentation process. They are the opposite of sweet wines, which have a higher residual sugar content.

Sweet wine

Sweet wine is a type of wine that has a higher residual sugar content than dry wines, resulting in a sweeter taste.

Sparkling wine

Known for its effervescence, sparkling wine can range from dry to sweet and is often associated with celebrations. Examples include Champagne, Prosecco, Cava, and Sparkling Rosé.

Fortified wine

Made by adding a distilled spirit, such as brandy, to the wine during the fermentation process, fortified wines are typically higher in alcohol content and can be sweet or dry. Examples include Sherry, Port, Madeira, and Marsala.

Dessert wine

Typically high in sugar and often enjoyed as a dessert or after-dinner drink, dessert wines can come in various styles such as late harvest, botrytized (noble rot), or ice wine.

Organic wine

Produced from grapes grown without the use of synthetic fertilizers, pesticides, or other chemicals, organic wines are made using organic farming practices.

Indigenous or regional wine

Produced from grape varieties that are native to a specific region or country, indigenous or regional wines highlight the unique flavors and characteristics of a particular terroir.

Blended wine

Made by blending different grape varieties or wines from different vineyards or regions, blended wines are created to achieve a desired flavor profile or balance.

Sparkling red wine

A unique type of wine that combines the effervescence of sparkling wine with the richness of red wine, resulting in a bubbly and flavorful red wine with a festive twist.
