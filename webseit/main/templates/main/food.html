What food goes with wine?
Red Meat

Red wines such as Cabernet Sauvignon, Merlot, and Syrah/Shiraz pair well with red meat dishes like steak, lamb, and beef stew. The tannins in red wines can help cut through the richness of the meat and enhance its flavors.

Poultry

White wines such as Chardonnay, Sauvignon Blanc, and Pinot Grigio can be great choices to pair with poultry dishes like chicken, turkey, and duck. The acidity in white wines can complement the lighter flavors of poultry.

Seafood

Seafood dishes like fish, shrimp, and scallops can pair well with white wines such as Sauvignon Blanc, Pinot Grigio, and Chardonnay. Lighter-bodied, crisp white wines can complement the delicate flavors of seafood.

Cheese

Cheeses can vary greatly in flavor and intensity, but some general guidelines for wine pairing include pairing soft, creamy cheeses like Brie or Camembert with white wines, and pairing harder, aged cheeses like Cheddar or Parmesan with red wines. However, experimentation is key, and personal taste preferences should also be taken into account.

Vegetarian Dishes

For vegetarian dishes, white wines with higher acidity, such as Sauvignon Blanc or Riesling, can work well with vegetable-based dishes, while light-bodied red wines like Pinot Noir or Beaujolais can complement dishes with mushrooms or roasted vegetables.

Desserts

Desserts can be paired with sweet wines like Moscato, Port, or late-harvest wines. The sweetness of the wine can complement the sweetness of the dessert, creating a harmonious balance.
